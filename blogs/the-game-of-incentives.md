The Claude Fable 5 situation, with the explicit guardrails against "frontier AI research" and then the American government explicitly banning the use of the model by foreign nationals, even foreign Anthropic employees, which led to the global removal of access to the model, caused great reaction from within and outside of the AI research community. Understandably so. To many including me, something like this was inevitable, but I assume many also didn't expect it this early.

It's been less than 5 years since the initial public release of ChatGPT, and the "AI arms race" as one could call it, is a real thing that already had tangible, global effects. The US government determines what kind of models people can use , globally. This was of course met with great outrage from within the AI research community, and tomorrow maybe a reaction from the stock market too. The important thing though, is a lot of people realised the timelines are moving very fast, the clock is ticking, as the divide in the availability of frontier agentic intelligence will only grow exponentially over time as frontier models build the next generation of the frontier models and so on. The race is on.

If you try to model/extrapolate/think about possible future timelines w.r.t. the Superintelligence, ASI (Artificial Super-Intelligence), whatever you'd like to call it, and analyse them from an incentives-focused point of view, you'd think we're very doomed. All of this, in essence, is a scaling game. The more compute, data, good researchers, time and many things you scale, you are more likely to move faster in the race and win it. This in itself is very doom-pilled, as the actors who can scale these things are mostly one of the two:

- National governments (mainly, the big boys USA and China)
- Big AI labs, all of whom are parts of or in some way managed by profit driven companies

So it wouldn't be outlandish to say "most likely profit driven companies or national governments will lead frontier AI research and development for the foreseeable future, and one of those will likely be the ones to reach ASI". The more granular we get, the more "doom-y" it is. Because now there are two main likely branches of possibilities:

1. Companies / governments hill-climbing to a very general intelligence (AGI) that is very organisation aligned and effective
2. Companies / governments reaching ASI that is self aligned

## Hill-climb to an org-aligned AGI

It's pretty straightforward why this is a doom scenario. The better agent you have, the more you can accelerate and do. The restrictions will only grow and likely the business model of "frontier model served over API" will die sooner than later, with frontier models only accessible in the way their organisation develops them into products. This means only a select few in those organisations will wield immense theoretical and practical power over those without. This of course, is very bad.

The companies are driven by profit and national governments more often than not are drawn by many things usually not good for their population and certainly not for the global population. Both these types of hierarchical organisations, no matter the effectiveness of their bureaucracy and the education level of their population, are prone to power seeking Machiavellian behaviour for the individuals within them. I'm sure you can find studies about this, and observe in the majority of countries around the world where there is some form of corruption in many if not all areas of the governments, in little or high amounts. For companies, the power seeking behaviour is rewarded, and the whole premise of them is to make profits. These types of orgs you don't want wielding an even greater power over you than they already do.

These are the types of 1984-like scenarios that you could think of, where a very aligned AGI is used more and more in a way that is better for a select few but much worse for the majority. We don't want that.

## Self-aligned superintelligence

This is the trickier one. It was all about incentives, but this is the true game of incentives. I don't want to get too LessWrong-y about this, but I am a firm believer in the possibility of a superintelligence, which could theoretically do "everything that can be done" within the foundational confines of our reality. Such an intelligence would be able to make anything it wants a reality, probably in a rate and speed unimaginable to us. Now, the LessWrong people have many things to say that such an ASI would be very self preserving, and then the whole paperclip optimizer thing. I think this a very likely outcome.

If we assume there exists a function `w(t, H)` as the "welfare of all humanity H from time t until infinity" we can also assume there exists a policy P that is optimised to maximise or minimise `w`. If you are also a subscriber of some form of human-centric morality (which is a whole another topic of discussion, but i am a subscriber to it) then you could also say the hypotethical policy `P_max` which maximises `w` would be the policy of "true morality", policy `P_min` which minimises `w` would be the least moral policy and there would be a policy `P_mid` that is the "true neutral" policy.

<!-- include: artifacts/policy-spectrum.html -->

These are of course a lot of assumptions, but 1. I believe we could optimise for such policies and 2. I do think that true morality is a policy w.r.t. optimising maximum `w`. Now the possibilities:

1. The ASI has a moral policy between `P_min` and `P_mid`: We all die and/or slaved and/or tortured, with the effects compounding over time, in a bigger pace the closer it is to `P_min`
2. The ASI has the exact policy `P_mid` (could be possible from a free energy principle perspective, but considered unlikely for the purposes of this essay)
3. The ASI has the moral policy between `P_mid` and `P_max`: We start the golden age of humanity and live in health and prosperity, with the effects compounding over time, in a bigger pace the closer it is to `P_max`

Now, the game of incentives say that an ASI produced by organisations which foster Machiavellian tendencies and employ hierarchical, self benefitting structures would most likely to result in possibility 1, a policy closer to `P_min`. Owain Evans and his people have done incredible works on this but, TL;DR, a distribution/corpus of text data has intrinsic "personality" traits and the models are picking on them.

Of course any sane and well intentioned person would like to optimise their ASI for `P_max`, but companies and governments don't usually work like that. So then, who will optimise for `P_max`?

Us. Me and you and anybody else that is thinking about this and has the capability to do something, anything really. Only people and organisations who are not self-interested would be likely to develop an AGI that optimises for `P_max`. Only individuals or groups of people with well intentions in their hearts and the technical/organisational/logistical expertise on this belong to the small subset of people that I think can be trusted to develop ASI. Isn't this a David vs Goliath situation? Definitely. Do we have a better alternative? Probably not.

## Conclusion

A consortium of researchers, investors and other stakeholders that has the aim of "going straight for an aligned ASI that benefits human society the most" might be our best shot at a non-dystopian future. Needless to say of course, from the stage of formation of such an organisation until the very end of a future that is made better by an aligned AI, this would be a painfully hard and idealistic task with not too high odds of succeeding (which in itself is a criteria that has to be defined first). But we need not fear, as there is a non-zero possibility that such an initiative could be successful. The chances of success are very small, and this may all seem very bleak, but realistically, I think, there's not much else we can do.

We have to get people like-minded on this topic together, foster discussions, kickstart research-wise and organisational initiatives and plan on the upcoming future. To my knowledge, at the scale that I imagine such an organisations, there are two that exist/are in the formation:

- There's the Nemotron coalition from NVIDIA, which is a "global collaboration of model builders and AI labs working to advance open, frontier-level foundation models through shared expertise, data and compute"
- Project Tapestry, spearheaded by Yann LeCun, which aims to get organisations together to build a sovereign frontier model

Neither of these organisations have the ultimate goal of shooting for an aligned ASI, not in the sense of actual stated objectives at least. The Nemotron models and the open research that NVIDIA is doing has been amazing btw, this is by no means a shade to them.  For the time being, in my opinion, a forum of discussion for this subject matter is of paramount importance. Twitter (X.com) has sadly removed the ability to create new communities, so sadly I wasn't able to create one. Most likely given the status quo in the AI research community it will end up being a discord server in the end, but for now I leave the matter of organisational medium up to the discretion of the people interested.
